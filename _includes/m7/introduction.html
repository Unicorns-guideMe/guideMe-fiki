Based on the form and interview answers we built the MVP, which can be downloaded here . It doesn't appear in Chrome Store yet as we have to wait for it to be reviewed, but we published it on our Landing Page .

A recurrent answer in the form we created was that people save their links in bookmarks/notes/locally, therefore we considered that storing and centralizing links to resources in a straightforward manner is important and added this functionality.

Many people (in form and interview) said that they like to organize their resources in folders, by subject. Our extension allows the user to tag saved links with one or multiple tags and visualize them in directories that contain these tags.

For some, the difficulty of the information explained is an important aspect, so we also added the functionality to filter search results or evaluate some content from this perspective.

One of the main factors that differentiate good resources from bad ones are other people's reviews, so we implemented a simple recommendation system based on ratings from other users who searched the same topic.
